# Cycle of Distrust

## Definition (ENCODE D3.4)

A feedback loop in which emotionally manipulative narratives erode institutional trust, which in turn increases citizens' susceptibility to further emotional manipulation and polarising content.

## Mechanism

1. **Emotionally charged disinformation** activates fear, anger and perceived threat
2. These emotions **reduce trust** in institutions, media and democratic processes
3. Reduced trust makes citizens **more vulnerable** to alternative narratives
4. Alternative narratives **further amplify** emotional responses
5. Cycle continues, deepening polarisation

## Breaking the Cycle (ENCODE Proposals)

- **Slow scrolling** — reflective pause before emotional engagement
- **Emotional literacy** — helping citizens recognise emotional manipulation
- **Transparent moderation** — reducing perceptions of institutional bias
- **Citizen Innovation Labs** — structured spaces for emotional meaning-making
