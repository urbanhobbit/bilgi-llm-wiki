# Affective Polarisation

## Definition (ENCODE Framework)

Affective polarisation refers to the emotional alignment of citizens along in-group/out-group lines, where political opponents are perceived not merely as holding different views but as fundamentally distant, untrustworthy or threatening. Unlike ideological polarisation, it is driven by emotional responses — anger, fear and distrust.

## Key Findings from ENCODE D3.4

- Polarisation more pronounced in **comment sections** than original posts
- Comments concentrated anger and hate, lacking structured value-based reasoning
- Politicians deploy a **bimodal emotional strategy**: happiness/enthusiasm + fear/anxiety, suppressing overt anger
- **Sarcasm** replaces counterargument with ridicule, harder to detect algorithmically
- During **electoral periods**, anger declines and happiness rises — distinct communicative regime

## Related Mechanisms

- **Emotional amplification loops:** high-arousal emotions drive engagement and algorithmic visibility
- **Cycle of Distrust:** emotional manipulation → polarisation → institutional distrust
- **Narrative plausibility bias:** users accept emotionally congruent narratives

## Policy Implications

- Traditional fact-checking insufficient to address emotional drivers
- Need for Emotional Impact Assessments (EIAs) for platforms
- Emotional literacy as a democratic competence
