# Emotional Amplification Loops

## Definition

The mechanism by which platform algorithms designed to maximise engagement systematically amplify high-arousal emotional content (anger, fear, outrage), creating a feedback loop that drives polarisation while increasing user attention.

## ENCODE Evidence

- Emotionally charged content generated **substantially higher engagement** than neutral content
- **Negative emotions** (anger, fear) most prevalent across the dataset
- Strong correlation between fear and security-oriented values (22% above chance)
- Algorithmic systems that prioritise engagement amplify emotionally intense content, distorting perceptions of public opinion

## Proposed Interventions

- Adjustments to recommendation systems prioritising extreme emotional reactions
- "Slow scrolling" friction mechanisms before sharing
- Transparency requirements for algorithmic amplification patterns
- Emotional Impact Assessments (EIAs) as regulatory tool

## DSA Integration

The ENCODE catalogue recommends integrating emotional amplification monitoring into the DSA's systemic risk assessment obligations for VLOPs.
